✦ Overview Insight

The Rumonge community demonstrates strong social cohesion and faith resilience, with 85% feeling safe locally and 77% reporting growing faith. However, the survey reveals a critical livelihood stress: 74% of households faced food insecurity in the past month, and 40% of income earners are earning less than last year. Water, sanitation, and income/employment top community priority requests.

✅ Community Strengths

  • Strong faith resilience — 64% report faith growing, acting as primary coping mechanism
  • High community safety — 85% feel safe and protected locally
  • Social cohesion — 68% in community groups; 73% have support networks
  • Optimism and hope — 76% feel hopeful about the future despite challenges
  • Near-universal sanitation — 99% have toilet access; 73% use piped water
  • High household agency — 75% believe they can create change in their own life

⚠️ Critical Challenges

  • Severe food insecurity — 74% of households lack consistent daily food
  • Declining incomes — 40% earning less than last year; precarious informal work
  • High illness rates — 59% suffered illness in the past month
  • Healthcare access barriers — 33% face barriers, primarily cost-related
  • Climate vulnerability — 85% experienced crop loss; drought and flooding concurrent
  • Low formal education — 32% have no formal education, limiting livelihood options

🎯 Recommended Priority Actions

  • Scale income generation programmes targeting small trade & agricultural value chains
  • Expand climate-smart agriculture training to reduce crop loss vulnerability
  • Strengthen community health funds / insurance mechanisms to reduce cost barriers
  • Invest in water quality improvements — 73% depend on public taps with variable safety
  • Design women-focused wellbeing interventions to close the 0.41-point gender gap
  • Leverage existing faith & community group networks for programme delivery

📊 Monitoring Indicators

  • Track WHO-5 mean score (currently 3.21 avg) — aim for 3.5+ within 12 months
  • Reduce food insecurity from 74% → 60% within one agricultural cycle
  • Increase savings rate from 44% → 55% of households
  • Reduce healthcare barrier rate from 33% → 20%
  • Increase community group participation from 68% → 75%
  • Track crop loss rate (currently 85%) as key climate adaptation metric

✦ Overall Assessment

The Rumonge community shows a paradox of resilience under stress: exceptionally strong social, spiritual, and psychological indicators coexist with acute material hardship. The Light Wheel Shalom programme operates in fertile ground — trust is high, community networks are active, and faith provides a powerful motivational framework. The most urgent interventions should address the food–income–climate nexus, where crop loss, declining earnings, and food insecurity form a reinforcing cycle. Programmes that combine livelihood support with climate adaptation stand the best chance of breaking this cycle.
